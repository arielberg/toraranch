About Us אודותינו

As we all know, Am Yisrael and the State of Israel are facing major challenges today. The Halutz program has chosen to rise from today’s crises and embark upon a new endeavor to cultivate the Jewish leaders of tomorrow; those who will strengthen our Nation and our presence in the Land, and become the "pioneers" of tomorrow in all aspects of life. כידוע, עם ישראל ומדינת ישראל מתמודדות היום עם אתגרים גדולים. תכנית חלוץ בחרה להתרומם מהיום משברים ויציאה למאמץ חדש לטפח את המנהיגים היהודים של המחר; אלה שיחזקו את אומתנו ואת נוכחותנו בארץ ויהפכו ה"חלוצים" של המחר בכל היבטי החיים.

Our Vision

The Halutz program prepares its participants for the challenges of adult Jewish life today, creating a new cadre of ‘pioneers’ that will see themselves leading our People in a variety of fields, based on in-depth Torah understandings and a living connection to the Land of Israel developed by actually working the land. The program encourages not only that we study Torah, but that we apply Torah both on the personal level and on the national level. By combining a living Torah together with other fields of knowledge and professional training in a variety of trades and practical work in agriculture and land development, the program aims to create a unique individual who will be equipped to face the challenges of the next generation.

How Does It Work?

Halutz participants will be afforded the opportunity to participate as pioneers building our homeland, while engaging intimately with those Israelis most passionately committed to the story of the Jewish people. The daily schedule includes regular study hours in the Beit Midrash, the goal of which is to enable every young man to take an active part in understanding and clarifying for himself our Torah and the Jewish faith, while applying insights together with their mentors to meet the challenges of the day. By living, working, and studying alongside craftsmen, vintners, shepherds and farmers, students will learn new skills and develop their connection to today’s Jewish renaissance, actually creating, building, and working the Land while deepening their commitment to our people’s rich heritage. The program operates on an apprenticeship basis, offering seminars in fields that include woodworking, stone-masonry, welding, organic farming, tanning, shepherding, wine-making and more, linking these crafts to the powerful spiritual teachings and traditional values of the People of Israel. Participants will experience the entire process of manufacture and production from raw materials to the creation of magnificent artworks, gaining practical tools to be leaders of the next generation. In-the-field learning will be augmented with lectures and workshops ranging from Torah lessons to topics like Middle East history and geopolitics. In addition, there will be a common fund with which the students of the Kedem Academy will finance enrichment activities to prepare them for leading roles in the Israeli military, including: Guided tours. Field workshops. Workshops to get to know Israeli society. Navigation and topography studies. MDS and physical and mental preparation for military service. External lessons of role models (Rabbis, heroes of Israel, pioneers, etc.) Diverse social activity

Location

The Halutz program is a new educational project situated on the Pnei Kedem Farm, in southeast Gush Etzion on the edge of the Judean desert, overlooking the Dead Sea, above the Arugot stream that runs all the way down to the famous springs of Ein Gedi. The farm itself controls thousands of acres of State Land, raises sheep and goats, has a variety of wine vineyards and other agricultural endeavors. The farm is committed to redeeming and developing the Land, beautifying it with hiking trails, observation points, resting places and other tourist attractions. Halutz participants live, study, and work on the farm, develop the Land, and receive practical tools to deal with the various challenges that life on a pioneering farm invites. The relative remoteness of the farm and the spaces around it constitute an ideal platform for an educational process that will grow the leaders of tomorrow.

Program

What is it?

A program that affords young adults the opportunity to participate as pioneers in the building of our homeland, while engaging intimately with those Israelis most passionately committed to the living story of the Jewish People. Participants live, work and learn alongside craftsmen and agriculturalists on our farm, learn new skills and develop their connection to today's Jewish renaissance, actually creating, building and working the land. Participants receive personal guidance from dedicated staff members, who assist them in developing their own unique and authentic path.

How long is it?

Most participants stay from three months to a year. We recommend no shorter than a two month stay.

How much does it cost?

Room and board costs $1400 per month, check with us for possible scholarships/ funding.

When does it start?

The program runs throughout the year, and is flexible to the needs of its participants. Please contact us in order to arrange the best starting time for you.

Who is it for?

The Halutz program is specifically geared towards young adults and couples who: Enjoy learning new skills in an apprenticeship-style workshop environment... Want to take actual part in the Jewish renaissance taking place Are independent and self-motivated Enjoy integrating fulfilling work with powerful teachings ranging from Torah to geopolitics Enjoy unique journeys, and don't mind working hard and getting their hands dirty! Understand the value of developing ourselves as holistic, integrated individuals, mind and matter, spiritual and material, for all is One. Workshops

Our Team

Gavriel Reiss Founder and director of the Pnei Kedem Farms, born and raised in Los Angeles, served in elite units in the Israeli army, holds a triple degree in philosophy, political science and economics from the Hebrew University, as well as a letter of yoreh yoreh. Ori Arnon, born and raised in the Jewish quarter of Hebron, is the General Director of the Kedem Pre-Military Academy. A longtime resident of Pnei Kedem, Ori has a Master's degree in education and Eretz Israel; he is an experienced educator, tour guide and project manager.
